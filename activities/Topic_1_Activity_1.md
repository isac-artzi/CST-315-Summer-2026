# Topic 1 · Activity 1 — Sketch Your Computer as a Factory

**Course:** CST-315 Operating Systems Lecture & Lab
**Duration:** ~20 minutes
**Format:** Individual sketch, then pair share

## Aligned objectives
1. Demonstrate familiarity with the features of a UNIX/Linux operating system.
5. Solve classical and key problems associated with the design and functionality of operating systems.
7. Establish efficient team processes that prioritize goal setting, task management, and problem-solving within the team.

## What you will produce
A one-page hand-drawn diagram of the operating system as a factory, plus a short written narrative that walks one familiar action ("I double-clicked an app icon") through the zones of your drawing.

## Setup
- A blank sheet of paper (or the printed floor-plan worksheet).
- The master visualization projected at the front of the room (`Factory_OS_Master.html`).
- Pen or pencil. No laptop needed for this activity.

## Steps

1. **Watch the tour (5 min).** The instructor walks the master visualization, naming each zone aloud and pointing at the corresponding OS component. Listen — do not draw yet.

2. **Sketch (10 min).** On your paper, draw a simplified version of the factory. Label every zone you remember:
   - Front Desk (Shell)
   - Floor (CPU + Scheduler)
   - Staging Racks (RAM)
   - Off-Site Warehouse (Virtual Memory)
   - Records Room (File System)
   - Loading Dock (I/O)
   - Manager's Office (Kernel)
   - Mini-Factories (VMs)

   Then write one or two sentences below the drawing answering: *"What happens, behind the scenes, when I double-click an icon to open an app?"* Trace the action through your drawn factory — name each zone the action visits, in order.

3. **Pair share (5 min).** Pair up with a classmate. Swap papers. Compare your two narratives. Where do they diverge? Which zones did each of you forget?

## Discussion prompts
- Which zone was hardest to draw or explain? Why?
- Where in your factory does the answer to "where did my window go when I closed it" live?
- The master diagram shows nine zones. How many did you actually involve in your double-click story? What does that suggest about how invisible most of the OS is?

## Stretch goal
Add a second story to your sheet: *"What happens when my browser runs out of memory and starts swapping?"* Trace this through the warehouse zone. We will revisit this in Topic 4.
